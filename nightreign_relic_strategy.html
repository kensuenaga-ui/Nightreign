ELDEN RING NIGHTREIGN

Relic Strategy Guide

Core principles and empirical findings for Deep of Night relic optimization. Compiled by Ken from extensive in-game testing across Revenant and Executor builds — not community consensus, but personal research verified against live data.
01 — Foundation

The Two Damage Pipelines

Nightreign has two completely separate damage calculation systems. Understanding which one your build uses determines everything about how you stack relics.

Weapon Pipeline

For melee attacks, infused weapon swings, and weapon arts.

Calculation Order

Base Weapon AR → Infusion/Buff → Affinity Attack Power × Melee × Skill × Physical × Character-Specific

  • Improved Affinity Attack Power CONFIRMED — separate multiplier on Flaming Strike Cursed Claws build (30+ damage gain over Physical +4)
  • "Boosts Attack Power of Added Affinity Attacks" — explicitly for weapon infusions and greases only, does NOT affect incantations

Incantation Pipeline

For spells cast from a seal. Different calculation, different available buckets.

Calculation Order

Seal Scaling × FTH → Spell Base → [Damage Type + Spell School] × Improved Incantations × Affinity*

* Affinity is school-dependent — see Bucket Rules

  • Improved Affinity Attack Power is school-dependent — works on Giants' Flame (O Flame), does NOT work on Erdtree Worship (Wrath of Gold)
  • Improved Incantations CONFIRMED — separate multiplier from damage type/spell school (30 damage gain on fire build)
02 — Core Mechanic

Damage Bucket Rules

Effects within the same bucket ADD together. Effects across different buckets MULTIPLY together. Spreading across buckets gives more damage than stacking one bucket — but only when the effects being compared are similar size.

Confirmed Same-Bucket Pairs

SAME BUCKET Physical Attack Up + Improved Bestial Incantations — swapping one for the other gave 1-point difference TESTED
SAME BUCKET Fire Attack Power Up + Improved Giants' Flame — strongly suspected based on Bestial precedent SUSPECTED

Pattern: damage type and spell school effects are likely one additive bucket for the incantation pipeline.

Confirmed Separate Buckets — Incantation Pipeline

BUCKET 1 Damage Type (Fire Attack Up) + Spell School (Giants' Flame) — additive within
BUCKET 2 Improved Incantations — +30 damage gain proves separate multiplier CONFIRMED
BUCKET 3 Improved Affinity Attack Power — +30 damage loss when removed on Giants' Flame CONFIRMED

Confirmed Separate Buckets — Weapon Pipeline

BUCKET A Improved Melee Attack Power CONFIRMED
BUCKET B Improved Skill Attack Power — both Melee + Skill apply to Cursed Sword post-1.03.2 CONFIRMED
BUCKET C Improved Affinity Attack Power — outperformed Physical +4 on Flaming Strike build CONFIRMED
BUCKET D Physical Attack Up — likely separate from Melee/Skill UNTESTED
BUCKET E [Executor] Skill Boosts Attack — +35% damage, -40% negation while attacking. Synergizes with "Nullify Attacks When Negation is Lowered" CONFIRMED

Dead Effects by Pipeline

DEAD Improved Affinity Attack Power on Erdtree Worship incantations (Wrath of Gold, Discus of Light)
DEAD "Boosts Attack Power of Added Affinity Attacks" on any incantation — weapon pipeline only
DEAD Katana-specific relics on Cursed Sword — different weapon classification
03 — Deep Relic Efficiency

The +3 Sweet Spot Rule

Deep relic damage type tiers, datamined from regulation.bin via Nexus Mods:

TierValueJumpDemerit
+03%—None
+14%+1%None
+25%+1%None (standard cap)
+38.5%+3.5%Mild or none
+410%+1.5%Always harsh
+3 is the efficiency king. 85% of +4's value, significantly milder demerits. The +2→+3 jump (3.5%) is the biggest single-tier gain. The +3→+4 jump (1.5%) rarely justifies the mandatory curse.

Exception: When a +4 has a self-offsetting demerit (e.g., Sleep Buildup + Sleep Resistance on the same relic), the demerit cost is zero and the +4 is free value.

05 — Advanced

Spreading vs Stacking

The bucket spreading rule has a breakpoint. When one effect is significantly larger than the alternatives, brute-force stacking can beat multiplicative spreading.

Spread — 511 DMG ✓

Fire +4 + Giants' Flame ×2 + Fire +2 + Fire +1
+ Improved Incantations +2
+ Affinity Attack +2

Demerits: 2 mild, self-managed

Stack — 530 DMG ✗

Same bucket 1
+ THREE Fire +4 replacing buckets 2 & 3

Demerits: 5 harsh, double unresisted sleep

19 more damage isn't worth dying to sleep mid-Nightlord. Spread across buckets when effects are similar size. Stack the biggest effect only when demerits stay manageable. Always weigh the curse against the gain.
06 — Slot Strategy

Standard vs Deep Relics

Standard Relics (Slots 1–3)

  • Cap at +2 tier (only 5% for damage types)
  • Spell school effects are stronger than +2 damage type — Giants' Flame gives 12% vs Fire +2's 5%. Always prioritize spell school in standard slots.
  • Giants' Flame confirmed stronger than Fire +2 in direct swap — drove O Flame from 460 to 484
  • Stacking multiple spell school effects is optimal (double Giants' Flame = +27 damage on fire build)
  • White vessel slots accept any color — this unlocked a second Giants' Flame from a Yellow relic in a White standard slot

Deep Relics (Slots 4–6)

  • Access +3 (8.5%) and +4 (10%) tiers — the only place damage type effects outperform spell school
  • +3 is the sweet spot for efficiency
  • Always check ALL demerits — CSV exports only show first 3 effects, deep relics can have 4-6. Screenshot in-game before committing.
  • Character-specific effects (Revenant FP, Executor skill boost) can be irreplaceable — don't sacrifice them for marginal DPS
07 — Vessels

Vessel Optimization

Color constraint is the real bottleneck. The best relic in your collection might not fit your vessel's color slots.

  • Always check if rearranging which bucket goes in which color slot improves companion effects — same DPS, different demerits
  • White (wild card) slots are extremely valuable — they access your entire collection for that slot
  • Don't forget standard slot constraints — Starting Spell relics are color-locked and non-negotiable
Vessel Selection Process
Identify your 3 non-negotiable DPS effects (one per bucket)
Check which colors each effect is available in at highest viable tier
Find the vessel whose color configuration gives the best match with mildest demerits
Verify standard slot constraints — your Starting Spell color determines vessel eligibility
08 — Process

Build Optimization Checklist

Identify your damage pipeline — weapon or incantation? This determines which buckets exist.
List available buckets — some spells lack a matching school boost (Erdtree Worship has none). Don't assume 3 buckets; verify.
Get one effect in each bucket first — before stacking duplicates in any single bucket.
Prefer +3 deep relics over +4 — unless the +4 has clean companions or a self-offsetting demerit.
Check ALL demerits in-game — not from CSV exports. Deep relics hide effects past position 3.
Evaluate companion effects — a +3 with useful utility beats a +4 with dead weight and a harsh curse.
Check vessel color compatibility — consider rearranging bucket-to-slot assignment for better demerits.
Don't forget the White slot — it might unlock a stronger off-color relic you've been overlooking.
