Build Note Starting Weapon End-Run Viability

Most starting weapons fall off in damage by mid-to-late run. Only four characters have strong reasons to keep theirs through the Nightlord fight.

Character Keep starting weapon? Why
Executor Yes — core build Executor's Blade has guaranteed innate bleed buildup, which no random katana drop can be assumed to have. The weapon's type also enables the full Arcane-scaling bleed build from Day 1 without relying on RNG drops. Relic investment in the starting weapon is standard practice.
Ironeye Yes — no alternative category Ironeye's entire relic kit is built around bows. Finding a better bow in-run is possible (Serpent Bow, Black Bow), but keeping the starting bow upgraded is universally endorsed as viable for the full run, especially with imbue and skill relics that target it specifically.
Duchess Yes — relic synergy + powerstance Duchess's dagger relic effects are tuned specifically to her starting weapon type. The powerstance dagger playstyle is central to her damage loop (Restage doubles recently-dealt damage), and her relic effects targeting dagger attacks mean upgrading the starting weapon is often more valuable than swapping to a different weapon class.
Revenant Contested — situationally yes Community is split. The Cursed Claws have Physical + Magic split damage and utility (long reach for teammate revival). With imbue relics and Holy damage conversion they remain viable. However, many experienced players consider them subpar by Day 2 and prefer replacing with a Faith-scaling weapon (Halo Scythe, Coded Sword) when available. The Finger Seal is always kept.
Wylder No — replace when found Generic greatsword with no unique properties. Better greatswords (Blasphemous Blade, Golden Order Greatsword, Marais Executioner's Sword) are far superior when found. Starting weapon is Day 1 only.
Guardian No — replace when found Standard halberd. Guardian's value is in his shield/guard kit, not weapon damage. Better options (Bolt of Gransax, Commander's Standard) found in-run.
Raider No — replace quickly Generic colossal axe. Raider benefits most from Colossal Weapons or Greataxes with status effects or unique skills. Starting weapon is functional but has no unique advantages to lock in.
Recluse Staff — keep and upgrade Not applicable in the same sense — Recluse keeps her staff for spells, not melee damage. Finding a higher-tier staff upgrades her output. Starting staff is Day 1 utility only.
Scholar No — replace when found Standard thrusting sword. Scholar's damage ceiling is with Arcane-scaling status thrusting swords or heavy thrusting swords found in-run. Starting weapon is functional early, drops off later.
Undertaker No — replace when found Standard hammer. Undertaker benefits most from Faith-scaling hammers or weapons with strong unique skills (Marika's Hammer). Starting weapon is Day 1 only.

Notes & Caveats

  • Revenant (Fist/Claw): Explicitly excluded from Chilling Mist, Poisonous Mist, Sacred Blade (fists/claws excepted). Also excluded from Seppuku, Blood Blade, Poison Moth Flight, Lightning Slash, Eruption, Glintblade Phalanx (not swords/axes/hammers). Compatible with: Endure, Quickstep, Storm Stomp, Determination, White Shadow's Lure, Flaming Strike, Thunderbolt, Hoarfrost Stomp.
  • Duchess (Dagger): Excluded from Seppuku (small weapons) and Gravitas (small armaments). All other skill effects compatible.
  • Raider (Greataxe): Excluded from Glintblade Phalanx, Seppuku, Blood Blade, Poison Moth Flight. Gains Prayerful Strike ✓ and Eruption ✓.
  • Guardian (Halberd): Excluded from Lightning Slash (confirmed on Fextralife), Eruption, Prayerful Strike, Blood Blade, Poison Moth Flight. Gains Seppuku ✓ (thrusting polearm).
  • Undertaker (Hammer): Excluded from Eruption — Eruption requires Great Hammers, not regular Hammers. Gains Prayerful Strike ✓ and Lightning Slash ✓.
  • Wylder (Greatsword): Excluded from Blood Blade and Poison Moth Flight (large sword, both require small/medium swords). Gains Seppuku ✓ and Eruption ✓.
  • Ironeye: Zero compatible skill effects except Rain of Arrows. Incompatible with all imbues and all spells. Rain of Arrows is his entire relic category.
  • Recluse: Incompatible with all skill and imbue effects — no melee weapon at start. Owns the sorcery spell column alone.
  • Prayerful Strike (Undertaker): Undertaker's starting weapon already has Prayerful Strike — this relic would only apply to a second equipped weapon, making it functionally redundant on Undertaker unless she picks up a second compatible weapon in-run.
  • Spell table: Image 3 was cut off after Dragonfire. Additional incantation entries may exist — the table reflects the 10 visible effects only. All additional entries would follow the same pattern: Revenant ✓, all others ✗.
  • Scholar and Undertaker are DLC-exclusive (Forsaken Hollows).
